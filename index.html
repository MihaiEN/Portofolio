Profile

Professional overview

A focused introduction to my work across mathematics teaching, curriculum development, assessment design, data-informed planning and AI-informed education.

I am a qualified mathematics teacher with PGCE and QTS, supported by a BSc (Hons) in Mathematics. My experience spans classroom teaching, online provision, private tutoring, curriculum development, assessment design and examiner-style quality assurance.

My work is centred on making mathematics clear, purposeful and academically rigorous. I focus on carefully sequenced explanations, precise modelling, meaningful practice and feedback that helps students understand what to improve next.

Alongside teaching and assessment, I bring a data-aware and AI-informed approach to education. I am interested in how learner progress data, digital tools and responsible AI practice can support better planning, clearer feedback and more targeted academic intervention.

Approach

How I work

The same principles run through my teaching, tutoring, assessment work, curriculum design and data-informed planning.

01

Clarity before complexity

I aim to make mathematical ideas feel structured and accessible through precise explanation, clean modelling and carefully chosen examples.

02

Assessment that reveals thinking

Good assessment should do more than check answers. It should reveal misconceptions, reasoning, confidence and the next steps for learning.

03

Evidence before assumption

Whether using student responses, assessment outcomes, progress data or AI-supported reflection, I try to make decisions from evidence rather than guesswork.
