Profile

Professional overview

A focused introduction to my work as a mathematics educator, assessment professional and data-informed practitioner.

I work across mathematics teaching, tutoring, assessment design and examiner-style quality assurance. My practice is built around clear explanation, careful sequencing, purposeful feedback and a strong understanding of how students prepare for examinations.

Alongside teaching, I bring a data-aware approach to education. I have experience using analytical tools and progress information to identify patterns, support planning and communicate findings clearly. This helps me connect classroom practice, assessment design and learner progress in a more informed way.

Approach

How I work

The same principles run through my teaching, tutoring, assessment work and data-informed planning.

01

Clarity first

I aim to make mathematical ideas feel structured and accessible through precise explanation, clean modelling and carefully chosen examples.

02

Assessment with purpose

I see assessment as more than checking answers. Good questions reveal thinking, misconceptions and the next steps for learning.

03

Evidence before assumption

Whether using learner responses, assessment outcomes or progress data, I try to make decisions from evidence rather than guesswork.
