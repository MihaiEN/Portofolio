Principles

Principles for teaching and assessment.

01

Make ideas precise

Careful notation, clear definitions and well-chosen examples help students engage with demanding ideas without removing their complexity.

02

Make thinking visible

A useful question should reveal reasoning, misconceptions and confidence rather than record only a final answer.

03

Use evidence purposefully

Written work, discussion and assessment outcomes should inform feedback, intervention and the next stage of teaching.

04

Use technology with judgement

Digital tools can support planning, explanation and reflection, but subject expertise, safeguarding and professional responsibility remain central.
